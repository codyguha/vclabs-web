About VC Labs

At VC Labs we are inspired by the potential and promise of communities. Communities are the way we connect, the way we learn, and the way we collectively drive positive action. Communities are powerful and when used effectively can add tremendous value to any modern agile business.

Getting the people together and talking is only half the battle. The real key to unlocking the success of any community is to focus on building a healthy community. More then ever it is absolutely critical to have active, engaged members that feel they have an appreciated and valued voice. Communities that are engaged, well informed, and active are the communities that people want to be a part of. At VC labs we recognize that all communities benefit from more engaged members and that engagement can be achieved by leveraging frictionless technologies - both existing and emerging. Think of VC labs as your technology partner, and together lets leverage techonlogy to work smarter and get the most out of your communities - together we will do amazing things.

Our Mission

At VC Labs our mission is to develop and deploy products and services that boost the value of communities. We build solutions that appeal to both community members and administrators. Good design is at the heart of our culture, and we take enourmous pride in balancing the utility of a solution with the usability, and desirablity that our partners expect. We strive to develop and deliver offerings that will rapidly amplify the membership experience and ultimately make your community a more effective and valuable tool.

Take Ten

From a user expeirence perspective Take Ten was designed to be light touch, cognitively simple, and fun. Our goal was to directly address the community onboarding engagement gap, and our task was to deliver a content experience that would make joining a new community more inspiring.

“Take Ten” was envisioned and developed as “Module 1” in a serise of value added “Survey Challenges” that an IC manager might turn on for their community. The challenges themselves are optional and meant to exist as experiences that the user can choose opt into.

To facilitate user experience testing the VC Labs team built a fully functioning iOS app. Our app approach made it easy to include rich interations and media, while affording the team with a native notification platform that was used to send “come back” reminders to the user. The screenshots below give an overview of the apps interaction model while providing a general sense of the overall Take Ten experience.

10 questions a day for 10 days
10 questions a day for 10 days One challenge with digital culture is that we have so many experiences competting for our attention. The outgrowth is that awareness and adoption can be very difficult outcomes to achieve. One strategy is to establish a simple but consistent interaction pattern that brings a user back in touch with an experience on a regular basis. Our 10 x 10 model paired with well designed notifications was an experiement in trying to establish this pattern of simple but consistent enagement. Each daily question set is presented around a simple theme. Daily question sets begin with a well defined introduction and end with a simple reward that communicates success and completion. The experience is meant to be short but consistently positive and fun.
Fun "this or that" style questions
Fun "This or That" Style Questions As consumers we spend so much of our day being assaulted by a relentless firehose of information. From an experience perspective this can get exhausting and it’s for this reason that the Take Ten questions themselves were design to be both visually engaging yet cognitively simple. Our binary “this or that” style quesions were purposefully designed to present as a breath of fresh air with in an otherwise chaotic stream of daily information.

Engaging touch points, outcomes, & recommendations
Engaging touch points, outcomes, & recommendations

One slightly surprising dimension to the Take Ten challenge is that its hosted by a charming animated monster. Experientially, this is a designed element that aims to push an emotional button and put a smile on the users face. Our goal was to make the user feel like they were building a relationship with the experience and the addition of a charming host was an experiment in trying to achieve that outcome.

Another important dimension to the challenge is the addition of outcomes and recommendations upon completing a daily set of questions. Upon completing the music set, for example, our host reports back to the user with a taste profile and then makes a recommendation for an album they might like based on the way they answered the questions.
